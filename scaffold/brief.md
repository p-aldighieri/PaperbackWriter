# Brief — `<paper slug>`

One paragraph, ~150 words. The roles read this as their primary input alongside `STRUCTURE.md` and whatever lives in `proofs/`.

Cover:

- **The result.** What does the paper claim, in one sentence a colleague would understand?
- **Who should care.** What economic question or open problem this addresses.
- **The contribution boundary.** What's *not* in this paper (so motivation-writer doesn't overstate).
- **Comparison anchors.** 3-6 papers the result is most directly in conversation with. Citation keys, comma-separated. The related-lit-writer uses this as a seed set.

Example skeleton:

> We characterize the optimal information structure when a designer wants to influence an agent who has private information about her own preferences. We show that … (key claim). This is interesting because (open problem). We do not address (boundary). Most closely related: `kamenica2011bayesian`, `lipnowski2020cheap`, `kolotilin2017persuasion`.

Keep it short. If you can't write the brief, you can't write the paper.
